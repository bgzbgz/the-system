# Fast Track Brand Guidelines

> **Status:** Internal Use Only
> **Source:** Fast Track Brand Guidelines (Confidential)

---

## 1. Brand Foundations

### 1.1 Business Goal

Fast Track exists to **disrupt and shake up** the traditional management consultancy and soft life‑coaching markets. The ambition is speed and effectiveness: simple, efficient, elegant tools and techniques that help companies and individuals grow **as fast as possible**.

This is not incremental improvement. It is decisive transformation.

---

### 1.2 Core Principles

These principles are non‑negotiable. They define how Fast Track behaves, decides, communicates, and delivers.

#### Brutal Honesty

* No sugar‑coating.
* Truth over comfort.
* Say what needs to be said, directly and clearly.

#### Obsessive 80/20

* 20% of inputs drive 80% of outcomes.
* Ruthless prioritisation.
* Eliminate noise, distractions, and vanity work.

#### Die Empty

* Full commitment or nothing.
* Expect total effort from Fast Track and from clients.
* Energy, intensity, and ownership until the job is done.

---

### 1.3 Audience

Fast Track works with **exceptionally ambitious individuals**, typically leaders and founders who:

* Are determined and growth‑oriented
* Value time as their most precious asset
* Invest in people
* Link personal growth directly to business performance
* Are proactive, disciplined, and value‑driven
* Believe comfort is the enemy of progress

---

### 1.4 Insight

> *Time is the most precious resource we have, but without discipline we waste it on things that don’t move the needle.*

Fast Track exists to restore discipline, clarity, and direction.

---

### 1.5 Brand Character

Fast Track has a strong, distinctive personality:

* **Focused** – ruthless clarity on what matters
* **Personal** – business is always personal; leadership is the lever
* **Challenging** – not hand‑holders or cheerleaders; progress comes from tension

---

### 1.6 Positioning

> **The battle‑hardened entrepreneurs who select the most ambitious individuals and push them beyond their limits.**

Fast Track is not for everyone. It is intentionally divisive.

---

### 1.7 Purpose

> **Empowering you to achieve what you thought was impossible.**

---

### 1.8 Brand House (Summary)

* **Business Goal:** Shake up consultancy & coaching

* **Principles:** Brutal Honesty · Obsessive 80/20 · Die Empty

Fast Track believes:

## 2. Tone of Voice

### 2.1 Overall Philosophy

Tone of voice is as important as visual identity. Every sentence signals:

* Confidence
* Authority
* Intent

> One well‑written sentence is better than a ton of bullshit.

---

### 2.2 Language Rules

**Do:**

* Keep sentences short and sharp
* Use active tense (“We do”, not “We will”)
* Write how you speak, but with discipline
* Use bold or colour for emphasis (never caps or exclamation marks)

**Avoid:**

* Rambling
* Repetition
* Flowery metaphors
* Gender‑based idioms
* Academic or fluffy language

---

### 2.3 How We Speak

Fast Track speaks:

* As a partner and equal
* With clarity and conviction
* Directly and honestly
* With energy, ambition, and purpose

Fast Track does **not** speak:

* As a subordinate or a bully
* Like a motivational poster
* Like a best friend who avoids the truth
* With cold detachment or ego

---

### 2.4 Tonal Dial

**Emphasise:**

* Confidence
* Power
* Sharpness
* Grit
* Charisma

**Minimise:**

* Arrogance
* Sexism
* Abstraction
* Repetition
* Hard‑sell tactics

---

### 2.5 Core Vocabulary

Words that belong to Fast Track:

* Fast
* Gritty
* Efficient
* Priority
* 80/20
* Brutal honesty
* Clarity
* Progress
* Stamina
* Die Empty
* Shoshin (Beginner’s Mind)

---

### 2.6 Signature Headlines

Examples of approved Fast Track phrasing:

* *No pressure, no diamonds.*
* *No grit, no growth.*
* *You call it impossible. We call it a brief.*
* *80% of your business is BS. We work on the 20% that isn’t.*

## 3. Visual Identity

## 3.1 Logo System

### Full Lockup

* Primary logo for first‑touch communications
* Bold, engineered typography
* Imposing and confident

### F Mark

* Distilled version of the logo
* Embodies the 80/20 principle
* Designed to become the primary mark once brand recognition is established

### Animation

* Horizontal bars sweep left → right
* Fast, decisive motion
* Continuous loop for energy and momentum

---

## 3.2 Colour System

### Primary Palette

Fast Track is predominantly **black and white**.

* **Black**
  RGB: 0,0,0
  HEX: #000000

* **White**
  RGB: 255,255,255
  HEX: #FFFFFF

### Secondary Palette

Used sparingly for emphasis.

* **Grey**
  RGB: 178,178,178
  HEX: #B2B2B2

* **Yellow (Accent)**
  RGB: 255,244,105
  HEX: #FFF469

> Photography provides most of the colour. Yellow is a precision tool, not decoration.

---

## 3.3 Typography

### Primary Typefaces

#### Headlines

* **Plaak Ex Condensed 43 Bold**
* Always uppercase
* Short, punchy statements only

#### Body Copy

* **Riforma LL Regular**
* Sentence case
* Clear, readable, disciplined

#### Annotations & Labels

* **Monument Grotesk Mono**
* Uppercase
* Used sparingly for labels, brackets, references

---

### 3.4 Typography Sizes (Key Rules)

**Presentations:**

* Page titles: Plaak 85pt
* Headlines: Plaak 360pt / leading 280pt
* Body copy: Riforma 45pt / leading 50pt

Consistency is critical. Never mix arbitrary sizes.

---

### 3.5 Dynamic Typography

* Repetition of words to create motion
* Used on covers and high‑impact moments
* Never overused
* Only a few words at a time

---

## 3.6 Layout & Grids

* 12‑column grid for landscape layouts
* Designed for large‑screen viewing
* Remove unnecessary micro‑details (e.g. page numbers)
* Strong left alignment

---

## 4. Art Direction

### 4.1 Visual Philosophy

Fast Track imagery must feel:

* Gritty
* Real
* Evocative
* Narrative‑driven

This is **not** a polished, corporate, digital‑first aesthetic.

---

### 4.2 Image Categories

#### Key Brand Images (What We Do)

* Used for first impressions
* High energy, transformation‑focused
* Saturated, colourful, grounded in reality

#### Supporting Images (How We Do)

* Process, preparation, tension
* Close‑ups and behind‑the‑scenes

#### Specialty Images (Why We Do)

* Conceptual
* Striking compositions
* Designed to make the viewer pause and think

---

### 4.3 Art Direction Do / Don’t

**Do:**

* Bold
* Provocative
* Grittily real
* Use material metaphors (fire, steel, motion)

**Don’t:**

* Slick or overly polished visuals
* Generic stock imagery
* Heavy CGI
* Overuse sports metaphors
* Illustrations (not yet defined)

---

## 5. Patterns & Motion

* Patterns are derived from the F mark
* Used **only in motion**, never static
* Serve as transitions
* Can use primary or secondary palette

---

## 6. Diagrams

* Inspired by technical graphs
* Flat, precise, crafted
* Art (40%) / Science (60%)
* Yellow used sparingly for emphasis
* Blended lines create movement

---

## 7. Applications

### 7.1 Presentations

* Black backgrounds or photography
* Strong headline‑image relationship
* Use builds for movement
* Vertical bars to create hierarchy

---

### 7.2 Implementation Tools (Workbooks)

* Predominantly white (fillable)
* Grey pages for learning sections
* White pages for exercises
* Yellow pages for checklists
* Generous space for writing

---

### 7.3 Digital Channels

**LinkedIn**

* Punchy headline + striking image
* Motion via pattern transitions

**Website**

* Bold, minimal landing pages
* Typography leads
* Animated transitions using patterns

---

### 7.4 Print

**Business Cards**

* Textured stock
* Balance grit and elegance

**Letterheads & Invites**

* Grey, white, and yellow stocks
* Foil and silkscreen finishes
* Avoid unnecessary ink usage

---

## 8. Final Principle

Fast Track is not decorative.
Every word, image, colour, and layout must earn its place.

**No grit. No growth.**

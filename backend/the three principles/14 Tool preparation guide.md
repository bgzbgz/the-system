**Sample Prompt for Creating a New Tool**

**Instruction**: You are an expert in [*Insert Relevant Theory or Topic*]. I want to create a practical tool to help teams apply this theory. Please create a user-friendly tool by following these guidelines:

1. **Clear Purpose & Objectives**: Provide a short description of what the tool will accomplish and why it’s important.
1. **Context & Relevance**: Illustrate real-world scenarios or challenges where this tool helps.
1. **User-Centric Design**: Outline the overall structure, sections, and layout in simple terms.
1. **Step-by-Step Instructions**: Describe how a team or individual should use this tool, step by step.
1. **Practical Examples & Illustrations**: Include at least one practical example or illustrative scenario.
1. **Flexibility & Adaptability**: Suggest any optional steps or customizations for different contexts.
1. **Evidence & Theory Integration**: Show how it aligns with [*Insert Relevant Concept or Theory*], and briefly explain the link.
1. **Outcome-Oriented Approach**: Indicate how success can be measured or observed.
1. **Guidance for Facilitators**: Provide tips or best practices for anyone introducing this tool to a team.
1. **Feedback & Continuous Improvement**: Suggest ways to collect feedback and improve the tool over time.
1. **Format Recommendation**: Offer suggestions on which format is best (Excel, PPT, Word, Interactive platform) and why.
1. **Design Structure & Content**: Provide clear instructions on design (sections, visuals, etc.) and content flow.

**Additional Context**: [*Include any other details about your specific needs, constraints, or the type of team using the tool.*]

Please ensure your answer clearly addresses each of these points with logical headings and an easy-to-follow structure. Thank you!

**How to Use This Guide**

1. **Have Your Theory Ready**: Decide on the management topic or concept you want to apply (e.g., conflict resolution, project prioritization).
1. **Insert Relevant Details**: Plug in the specific theory or scenario into the prompt template.
1. **Choose a Format**: Determine whether Excel, PPT, Word/PDF, or a collaborative platform fits best based on your team’s needs and technical comfort.
1. **Provide Any Constraints**: If you have a certain style, company culture, or design format, note this in the *Additional Context* section.
1. **Review & Adapt**: Take the response from ChatGPT (or any other AI) and tailor it to your organization’s style, language, and branding.
1. **Pilot & Gather Feedback**: Test the tool with a small group, gather their feedback, and refine accordingly.

By following these **10 requirements**, choosing the right **format**, and using the provided **sample prompt**, you’ll be able to consistently create **high-quality, practical tools** that empower teams to effectively apply management concepts in real-world settings.

Below is a **Tool Quality Checklist** you can give to ChatGPT (or any AI assistant) to evaluate the overall effectiveness, usability, and impact of a tool you’ve created. This checklist focuses on the **content**, **design**, and **practical value** of the tool itself—ensuring it meets the highest standards for user adoption and real-world application.

-----
**Tool Quality Checklist**

1. **Clear Purpose & Objectives**
   1. Does the tool explicitly state *what* it is designed to do and *why* it matters?
   1. Are the objectives specific and measurable?
1. **Context & Relevance**
   1. Is the tool aligned with real-world situations or challenges the users commonly face?
   1. Does it address a genuine need or problem in the workplace/project setting?
1. **User-Centric Design**
   1. Is the layout/structure intuitive and easy to navigate?
   1. Are all sections clearly labeled, and is the language accessible to the intended audience?
1. **Step-by-Step Instructions / Workflow**
   1. Does the tool include a logical sequence of actions (if needed)?
   1. Are instructions for using the tool integrated into or easily accessible within the tool itself?
1. **Practical Examples & Illustrations**
   1. Does the tool provide sample data, mock cases, or visual aids (diagrams, screenshots) that demonstrate how to use it?
   1. Do these examples reflect realistic scenarios?
1. **Flexibility & Adaptability**
   1. Can the tool be adjusted for different team sizes, project types, or organizational contexts?
   1. Are any optional features or customization tips clearly indicated?
1. **Evidence & Theory Integration**
   1. If the tool is based on a specific concept or theory, is that link clearly explained?
   1. Does it include brief references or summaries of the relevant theory/research?
1. **Outcome-Oriented Features**
   1. Does the tool help track progress, measure results, or facilitate clear outcomes (e.g., improved workflow, saved time)?
   1. Are there built-in indicators or metrics to show if users are on track?
1. **Guidance for Facilitators or Team Leaders**
   1. If the tool is used in a group setting, are there tips or notes for those introducing or guiding others in using it?
   1. Are there suggestions for follow-up activities or discussions?
1. **Feedback & Continuous Improvement**
- Does the tool have a mechanism (or guidance) for collecting feedback from users?
- Is there a clear process for updating or refining the tool based on user experience?
11. **Format & Technical Suitability**
- **Excel**: Are sheets/tabs labeled clearly? Are formulas locked or explained?
- **PowerPoint**: Are slides visually appealing, with minimal clutter and clear speaker notes if needed?
- **Word/PDF**: Is the text easy to scan (headings, bullet points)? Is it print-friendly?
- **Interactive Platforms**: Is navigation straightforward? Are collaborative elements well-structured?
12. **Overall Usability & Clarity**
- Is the tool straightforward enough that a first-time user can figure it out with minimal help?
- Is the content well-organized, and does it avoid unnecessary complexity or jargon?
